### Desolvation / Born effect

If one ionizable amino acid becomes buried inside the hydrophobic core, exposing charge to a hydrophobic environment is highly unfavorable. This shifts the residue toward an electrically neutral state, seen as a shift in pKa.
> 部分可电离aa会位于蛋白质内部疏水环境中。这使得其倾向于保持电中性状态，即pKa朝7偏移。

### Coulomb Interactions

When an ionizable amino acid is close to another charged species, the pairwise Coulomb interaction can affect its ionization state.
1. If ionization creates a charge **opposite** to the nearby species, the interaction is favorable and encourages ionization.
2. If ionization creates a charge of the **same sign**, the interaction is unfavorable and opposes ionization.
>aa电离后产生电荷与相邻物种相同，则电离被抑制（酸pKa↑，碱pKa↓）。反之亦然。

## (2) Mass Spectrometry
### Basic Principle of MS

Mass spectrometry measures the mass-to-charge ratio (m/z) of ions to identify and quantify molecules. The core process includes 4 key steps:

- **Ionization**: Convert neutral protein/peptide molecules into gas-phase ions
- **Mass Analysis**: Separate ions based on m/z ratio
- **Detection**: Measure the abundance of separated ions
- **Data Analysis**: Interpret ion signals to obtain molecular mass/sequence information

### 1. Ionization Methods for Protein/Peptide Analysis

1. The need for ionization
	Analyte has to be converted into gas-phase ions.
	Too much energy (hard ionisation) breaks peptides into smaller fragments.
2. **Electrospray Ionization (ESI，电喷雾电离)**
    
    - Soft ionization method (no fragmentation of intact proteins)
    - Principle: A high voltage is applied to a liquid stream containing analytes, producing charged droplets. Solvent evaporation leads to gas-phase ions.
     > ESI can generate multiply charged ions for large biomolecules (e.g., proteins), which reduces the m/z value to a detectable range.

	![[Pasted image 20260322165123.png]]
    
	- Application: LC/MS-MS
    
3. **Matrix-Assisted Laser Desorption/Ionization (MALDI，基质辅助激光解吸电离)**
    
    - Soft ionization method
    - Principle: Analytes are mixed with a matrix (e.g., α-cyano-4-hydroxycinnamic acid) and irradiated by a laser. The matrix absorbs laser energy, desorbing and ionizing the analytes.
     > MALDI is suitable for high-throughput analysis (e.g., peptide mass fingerprinting) and can analyze complex mixtures.
    
    ![[Pasted image 20260322163001.png]]
    - Application: Peptide mass fingerprinting (PMF), identification of protein digests
    

### 2. Mass Analyzers

| :------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------- |
| Time-of-Flight (TOF) | Ions travel through a flight tube; $m/z ∝ (flight time)²$                | Fast analysis<br>MALDI-TOF                                |

		二级质谱（MS/MS）：释放报告离子，实现定量

		    - 每个报告离子的质量唯一对应一个样本（如 114 → Sample 1，115 → Sample 2…）

### 2. Bragg Diffraction 
![[Screenshot_2026-03-22-20-28-13-693_com.newskyer.d.jpg]]

For crystals, diffraction arises from the scattering of X-rays by atoms arranged in a three-dimensional periodic structure. he scattered X-rays interfere with one another, and detectors record signals only where **constructive interference（相长干涉）** occurs.
>$$nλ=2dsinθ$$
>where:
	λ is the wavelength of the X-ray,
	d is the spacing between lattice planes,
	θ is the diffraction angle.
	
![[Pasted image 20260322221722.png]]

#### Why  X-rays
X-ray (Wavelength: 0.5–2.5 Å) diffraction patterns can reveal atomic arrangements only if the wavelength of the radiation is comparable to or smaller than the distance between atoms.

Diffraction pattern is actually a **Fourier Transform** of the electron distribution density, converts information from the spatial domain（空间域）into the frequency domain（频率域）. There is no lens that can directly focus X-rays into a molecular image. So the structure must be reconstructed computationally using X-ray diffraction data.
>可见光显微镜在成像过程中，物镜先对样品做傅里叶变换，把结构变成空间频率谱；成像透镜再做逆傅里叶变换，还原实空间图像；![[Pasted image 20260322222956.png]]
>然而X射线没有透镜调控波前(frontwave)，衍射后只能获得振幅信息而无法测得相位信息，无法直接得到晶体结构。

## (4) cyro-EM (Cryo-electron Microscopy，冷冻电镜)

Cryo-EM is a structural biology method used to determine the **3D structure** of macromolecules by imaging particles rapidly frozen in **vitreous ice（玻璃态冰）**. 
Resolution: 2 - 20+ Å
**Core Advantage** 
- Directly image macromolecules in a **near-native state
- Does **not require crystals**, and is especially 
- Suitable for **large molecular complexes**, **multiple conformational states**, and **heterogeneous samples（异质样品）**
> cryo-EM 的核心不是“先结晶再解结构”，而是 **把分子冻在接近天然的水环境里，直接拍很多张 2D 图，再用计算重建 3D**。

### 1. Vitrification（玻璃化冷冻）
The most critical step is **rapid freezing** of the aqueous sample at about **~10^6 °C/s**, so water does not crystallize and instead forms **amorphous / vitreous ice（无定形玻璃态冰）**. 
1. Add a small volume of sample onto an **EM grid**
2. **Blot** to make a thin liquid film
3. Plunge-freeze into liquid **ethane**
4. Keep the grid at **liquid nitrogen** temperature
### 2. Image Formation
Each micrograph is a **2D projection** of the original **3D object**. The 3D map must be reconstructed from many particle images recorded at different orientations.  
>cryo-EM image = noisy 2D projection of a 3D object.
#### Radiation Damage and Low-dose Imaging
Biological specimens are highly radiation-sensitive. High electron dose improves signal, but also destroys high-resolution information.
Radiation Damage vs. Signal Quality

### 3. Data Processing
#### Single-particle cryo-EM
In single-particle analysis, isolated macromolecular complexes are frozen in random orientations. The position and orientation of each particle must be determined computationally, then similar particle views are **grouped and averaged**. 
Mixed conformational states can also be separated by **classification**, sometimes described as **“purification in the computer”**.  
![[Pasted image 20260323010232.png]]
>Data processing workflow
>particle picking → classification → averaging → orientation determination → 3D starting model → projection matching / angular refinement → 3D reconstruction

Averaging similar particle views improves the **signal-to-noise ratio (SNR)**, which is why large particle numbers usually give better resolution.

## (2) Thermodynamics and Equilibrium
### 1. Gibbs Free Energy and Spontaneity
**ΔG**（非标准条件下自由能变化）: Gibbs free energy change; determines **reaction spontaneity**
$$ΔG=ΔH−TΔS$$
>一个过程是否自发，不只看放不放热（ΔH），还要看体系无序度（entropy, 熵）是否增加。
>吉布斯自由能在反应过程中随着反应程度进行不断变化
>ΔG < 0：forward spontaneous
>ΔG > 0：reverse spontaneous
>ΔG = 0：equilibrium
>ΔG The free energy of a reaction at any moment in time is therefore said to be a measure of the **energy available to do work**.
![[Pasted image 20260323164912.png]]

**ΔG°**（标准自由能变化）: Standard state: 298 K, 1 bar, 1 mol/L, free energy change for pure reactants → pure products at standard state.
>在**标准状态**下，所有反应物和产物的活度 / 浓度 / 分压都被定义为 **1**。
$$ΔG^∘=−RTlnK_{eq}​$$

$K_{eq}$（平衡常数）
> $K_{eq}>1$ → $ΔG°$ < 0 → product-favored, spontaneous
> $K_{eq} < 1$ → $ΔG°$ > 0 → reactant-favored, non-spontaneous
> $K_{eq} = 1$ → $ΔG°$ = 0 → at equilibrium

$$∆G = ∆G° + RT ln Q$$
>$R$ = 8.314 J/K·mole
>$Q$ = reaction quotient = $\frac {products}{reactants}$
>$Q < K_{eq}$ → forward spontaneous (toward product)
>$Q > K_{eq}$ → reverse spontaneous (toward reactant)
>$Q = K_{eq}$ → **equilibrium, ΔG = 0**

$$\Delta G 
= \Delta G^\circ + RT \ln \frac{[\text{ADP}][\text{P}_i]}{[\text{ATP}]}
= -7.3\ \text{kcal/mol} + RT \ln \frac{0.04\ \text{mM} \cdot 4.15\ \text{mM}}{3.75\ \text{mM}}
= -9.2\ \text{kcal/mol}
$$

>ATP ⟷ ADP + P𝑖: since the cellular concentration of ATP is much higher than that of ADP, the free energy of the reaction (Δ𝐺) is even more negative than Δ𝐺0.
### 2. The Second Law of Thermodynamics
The entropy of an isolated system remains constant or increases.
The total entropy of the universe is continually increasing.

### 3. Dissociation Constant (Kd)
For the reaction:  $$Protein (P)+Ligand (L)⇌Complex (PL)$$$$Kd​=\frac{[P][L]}{[PL]}, \space Ka=\frac{[PL]}{[P][L]}$$Kd (Unit: mol / liter) gives the concentration of ligand to saturate 50% of the ligand binding sites; almost all binding sites are saturated when ligand concentration is about 10 × Kd.
$$ΔG° = RT ln Kd$$Why 50%:
1. 解离常数定义及替换：
	$$K_d = \frac{[P][L]}{[PL]} = \frac{([P]_T - [PL])[L]}{[PL]}$$
2. 分数饱和度（结合位点占比）核心公式： 
	$$\frac{[PL]}{[P]_T} = \frac{[L]}{K_d + [L]}$$
3. 配体浓度守恒： 
	$$[L] = [L]_T - [PL]$$
4. 实验近似条件（总蛋白远小于 $K_d$）： 
	$$[P]_T \ll K_d \implies [L] \cong [L]_T$$
5. $$q \space (Fractional \space occupancy) = \frac{[PL]}{[P]_T​}​ = \frac{[L]_T}{Kd​+[L]_T}​$$
$[P]$：游离蛋白浓度
$[L]$：游离配体浓度
$[PL]$：蛋白-配体复合物浓度
$[P]_T$：总蛋白浓度（$[P]_T = [P] + [PL]$）
$[L]_T$：总配体浓度（$[L]_T = [L] + [PL]$）

$$\boxed{v=\frac{V_{max}[S]}{K_m+[S]}}$$

- $K_m$: Michaelis constant, the substrate concentration at $v_0=1/2V_{max}$

When \[S] >> Km, $V_0\approx V_{max}$, velocity becomes independent of \[S].

### (3) $k_{cat}$
$k_{cat}$: Turnover number, how many substrate molecules can one enzyme molecule convert per second.
$$k_{cat}=\frac{V_{max}}{[E]_T}$$

The final form in case of a single substrate is:
$$\boxed{v=\frac{k_{cat}[E]_T[S]}{K_m+[S]}}$$
### (4) Enzyme Efficiency and $k_{cat}/K_m$
This indicator combines catalytic speed and substrate affinity.
↑ → High efficiency
### (5) Inhibitors
Normal reaction:
$$E+S\rightleftharpoons ES\rightarrow E+P$$

- 看 **$E+S\rightleftharpoons ES$** 主要对应 **binding（结合）**
- 看 **$ES\rightarrow E+P$** 主要对应 **catalysis（催化）**

#### 1. Competitive inhibition
竞争性抑制：**Only binds E**, competes with substrate for the active site.
$$E+I\rightleftharpoons EI$$
主要影响：
$$E+S\rightleftharpoons ES$$
即抑制 **substrate binding**，不直接影响已经形成的 ES 去生成产物。

Modified Michaelis-Menten equation:
$$v=\frac{V_{max}[S]}{\alpha K_m+[S]}$$
where：
$$\alpha=1+\frac{[I]}{K_i}$$

- **$K_m \uparrow$**
- **$V_{max}$ unchanged**
![[Pasted image 20260324012226.png]]
>y轴交点不变

> 需要更高的底物浓度才能达到同样速率，因为底物和抑制剂在抢同一个位点；但高底物浓度下仍可达到原本的最大速率。

#### 2. Uncompetitive inhibition
反竞争性抑制：**Only binds ES**.
$$ES+I\rightleftharpoons ESI$$
主要影响：
$$ES\rightarrow E+P$$
即不阻止底物先结合，但会“锁住”ES，抑制 **catalysis**。

Modified Michaelis-Menten equation:
$$v=\frac{(V_{max}/\alpha')[S]}{(K_m/\alpha')+[S]}$$
where：
$$\alpha'=1+\frac{[I]}{K_i'}$$

- **$K_m \downarrow$**
- **$V_{max} \downarrow$**
![[Pasted image 20260324012312.png]]
>平行线

> 因为 inhibitor 稳定了 ES，所以表观上更容易形成 ES（$K_m$ 下降）；但一部分 ES 被困成 ESI，最大速率下降。**不能靠加底物克服。**

#### 3. Mixed inhibition
混合型抑制：**Binds both E and ES**.
$$E+I\rightleftharpoons EI$$
$$ES+I\rightleftharpoons ESI$$
主要同时影响：
$$E+S\rightleftharpoons ES$$
和
$$ES\rightarrow E+P$$

Modified Michaelis-Menten equation:
$$v=\frac{V_{max}[S]}{\alpha K_m+\alpha'[S]}$$

- **$V_{max} \downarrow$**
- **$K_m$ changes**
![[Pasted image 20260324012331.png]]
> mixed inhibition 同时影响 binding 和 catalysis。若 inhibitor 更偏爱 E，则表观 $K_m$ 往往升高；若更偏爱 ES，则表观 $K_m$ 往往降低。

#### 4. Noncompetitive inhibition
非竞争性抑制：**special case of mixed inhibition**.
即 inhibitor 对 **E** 和 **ES** 的结合程度相同。

Modified Michaelis-Menten equation:
$$v=\frac{(V_{max}/\alpha)[S]}{K_m+[S]}$$

- **$K_m$ unchanged**
- **$V_{max} \downarrow$**
![[Pasted image 20260324012435.png]]
>x轴交点不变

> noncompetitive inhibition 不明显改变底物结合能力，所以 $K_m$ 不变；但会降低酶的总体催化能力，因此 $V_{max}$ 下降。

#### Summary
- **Competitive**: binds **E** only → affects **binding** → $K_m\uparrow,\ V_{max}$ unchanged
- **Uncompetitive**: binds **ES** only → affects **catalysis after ES forms** → $K_m\downarrow,\ V_{max}\downarrow$
- **Mixed**: binds **E and ES** → affects **binding + catalysis** → $V_{max}\downarrow,\ K_m$ changes
- **Noncompetitive**: special case of mixed → $K_m$ unchanged, $V_{max}\downarrow$
