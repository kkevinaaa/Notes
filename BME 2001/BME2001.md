## 4. Membrane Transport

按是否需要能量分为两类：

| Types                | Energy    | Direction     | Example          |
| ----------------- | ----- | ------ | ----------- |
| Passive transport | No   | down a electro-chemical gradient | diffusion   |
| Active transport  | ATP | against (uphill) a electro-chemical gradient    | Na⁺-K⁺ pump |

### (1) Passive Transport

- Simple Diffusion
    * O₂
    * CO₂
    * steroid hormones (Hydrophobic molecules)

  - Osmosis
    > The net diffusion of water (the solvent) across the membrane.
    > Osmolarity is defined as the total number of solved particles per liter of  solution. Osmolarity of plasma is about 300 mOsm.

- Membrane protein mediated transport
  - Channel diffusion (Facilitated diffusion via channel)
      - Voltage gated
      - Ligand gated
      - Mechanical gated

  - Carrier-mediated transport
    > Saturation
    > Specificity
    > Competition
    - Facilitated diffusion via carrier

### (2) Active Transport

- Primary active transport
  > ATPase or pump
  - Na⁺-K⁺ pump (3Na⁺- out  2K⁺ - in)


- Secondary active transport
  > The energy stored in the form of ionic concentration gradient, created by primary active transport
  - Na⁺-glucose cotransporter
  - Co-transporter, antiporter, exchanger (等电荷/等摩尔)

### (3) Endocytosis & Exocytosis
- Phagocytosis 吞噬作用
- Pinocytosis  吞液
- Receptor-mediated

- Neurotransmitter

## 5. Bioelectricity

### (1) Resting Membrane Potential (RMP)
> ≈ −70 mV

- Origin
  - ==Potassium Diffusion Potential (Out)==
  - Sodium Diffusion (In)
  - Na⁺-K⁺ pump


- Nernst equation

  $E = \frac{RT}{zF} \ln \frac{[ion]_{out}}{[ion]_{in}}$
37℃:
  $E ≈ \frac{61}{z} \log \frac{[out]}{[in]}\quad \quad z$: 离子价态

- Goldman equation
  $E_m = 61 \log_{10} \frac{P_{\text{Na}^+}[\text{Na}^+]_{\text{out}} + P_{\text{K}^+}[\text{K}^+]_{\text{out}} + P_{\text{Cl}^-}[\text{Cl}^-]_{\text{in}}}{P_{\text{Na}^+}[\text{Na}^+]_{\text{in}} + P_{\text{K}^+}[\text{K}^+]_{\text{in}} + P_{\text{Cl}^-}[\text{Cl}^-]_{\text{out}}}$
